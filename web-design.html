Web Design

Brochure Websites

As mentioned on the homepage, brochure websites are lightweight websites. They are ideal for providing local businesses with a web presence as they do not rely on complex data management systems in the way websites like Facebook or Amazon do. Instead brochure websites function to showcase a business - providing key information, testimonials and updates. Brochure websites play a vital role for local business who conduct their trade in person. A recent survey reported that 76% of consumers look at online presence before physically visiting a business.

No Website Builders

The majority of today's websites are built using website builders, such as WordPress. These tools are fantastic for large scale or complicated website projects, but due to their cost, I believe they are completely unnecessary for brochure websites. The cheapest monthly WordPress subscription is €23, meaning owners of a WordPress website pay over €250 a year to simply own a website.

Web developers expense this cost to clients, passing it as the price to own a website, when in fact it is the price to own a website built using a building tool. Furthermore, this price is prior to paying for a custom domain registration or SSL security certificate, which may range between €30-120.

SX4 Websites

At SX4 all brochure websites are custom coded from scratch. By forgoing the use of a website builder, the websites I create avoid a hefty montly subscription fee. Furthermore, by skipping the use of website builders, SX4 websites recieve a free SSL secuirty certificate. This means all SX4 websites are HTTPS secure, as favoured by Google, free of charge. This saves customers €80-130 per year.

SX4 websites follow the "mobile first" design principle. This ensures all websites are user friendly on mobile, tablet and desktop devices. While the websites may be similar in terms of structure, it is the design that sets them apart.

My Approach

A common practice in web development is to carry out a design first approach with clients. From my experience, this approach leads to extended discussions over minor details, and typically favours developers who are paid by the hour.

My approach is unique in the manner that I fully create a website before reaching out to a business. This approach reduces an unnecessarily long design phase, saving both time and money. The main objective with a brochure website is to establish a web presence, and once this is achieved, design can then follow on a smaller scale.
